Cyberdystrophy

[noun] /saɪ.bər-   dɪs.trə.fi/

A progressive illness in which a person develops a dependence for the personal computer, until a recovery is no longer possible.

Cyberdystrophy is most common to start developing among young adults, teenagers and children, as most people over the age of thiry-five struggle to keep up with the development of modern technology.

Cyberdystrophy can take many forms, most of which, are fatal.
